FAQ

Questions founders ask first.

Everything you need to know about how Spine reads a hire — the gut-vs-Spine score, what your data is used for, how pricing works, and what happens after someone’s in the seat. Still stuck? Email lucas@withspine.co.

Product
What is Spine?
Spine is hiring decision intelligence for founders. At every interview stage it puts your gut read next to its own read of the candidate’s work record, surfaces the one risk worth watching before you decide, and then checks the call against reality at Day 30, 60, 90 and 180. It’s a compass for people decisions — not an applicant tracker, and not a sourcing tool.
How does the gut-vs-Spine scoring work?
At each stage you record where you stand in one tap — hire, maybe or pass, plus the one thing you’d watch. Your read is sealed before Spine’s opens, so neither colours the other. Spine then produces its own read from the candidate’s work record, scored across five dimensions: role-specific fit, execution speed, ownership, proactivity and resilience. Where the two lines agree, move fast; where they split, that gap is the interview question you haven’t asked yet.
What does “A-player probability” mean?
A-player probability is Spine’s single, calibrated estimate of how likely a candidate is to perform in the top tier of the role you’re hiring for — expressed as a percentage rather than a vague gut feeling. It’s built from the five-dimension read of the work record and weighted to what actually predicts success in that specific role, then sharpened over time as Day 30/60/90/180 outcomes feed back in. It’s a probability, not a verdict: it tells you how strong the bet is, so you can size the risk before you commit.
Is Spine a recruiter or sourcing tool?
No. Spine doesn’t source candidates or fill roles. It’s a decision layer that sits beside your existing process and instruments the decision itself — the read at each stage, the risk flags, the post-hire verification. You keep your own pipeline and your own recruiters; Spine makes the call sharper.
What is post-hire verification?
After a hire, the success picture agreed at offer is re-read at Day 30, 60, 90 and 180. Each check-in asks whether the prediction was right, whether the hire is on track, and what needs acting on while it’s still cheap to fix. At Day 180 you get a verdict — what you said, what Spine said, and what actually happened — which is what makes the next decision sharper than the last.
What does Spine integrate with?
Spine connects to the tools your hiring already runs through — Greenhouse, Lever, Gmail and Slack — so it can build your pipeline view without manual data entry. Connecting your stack is free; the integrations are how Spine assembles the work-record read and keeps each stage in sync.
Data & privacy
What data does Spine use, and is it GDPR compliant?
Spine reads the candidate’s documented work record — role history, what they’ve shipped, owned and carried — together with the structured notes from your own process. It’s built UK-first: ICO registered, GDPR compliant, and operating under the UK Data Protection Act 2018. Data is processed lawfully and held under strict access controls, and every flag cites its evidence, so a decision is never a black box.
Where does my data live and how is it secured?
Your data is held under strict access controls with encryption in transit and at rest, scoped so that each customer’s pipeline is isolated and visible only to the people you authorise. Spine is ICO registered and operates under the UK Data Protection Act 2018, processing data lawfully and retaining it only as long as needed for the decision and its post-hire verification. You can request export or deletion of your data at any time.
Do candidates get notified that Spine is used?
Spine is built for transparency. Where Spine processes a candidate’s data, the obligations under GDPR Article 14 apply, and we give you what you need to inform candidates fairly — including what data is used and why. Spine doesn’t make the hiring decision for you; it gives you an evidence-cited read, and every flag points to the record it came from, so the process stays explainable to the candidate and defensible to you.
Pricing & plans
How does pricing work?
Spine is free to sign up and integrate. You connect your tools and build your pipeline at no cost, and you only pay to unlock decision review and pipeline scoring once you’re integrated. Subscription tiers run from Entry at £195/month to Platform from £2,950/month, with annual billing saving 20%. You can cancel any time.
Is there a free trial?
Yes — the start is free. You can sign up, connect Greenhouse, Lever, Gmail or Slack, and build your pipeline view at no cost, then bring a live candidate and see Spine’s read next to your own before you pay anything. You only move to a paid tier when you want to unlock full decision review and pipeline scoring across your hires.
Can I cancel any time?
Yes. Spine is month-to-month with no lock-in — cancel any time. Annual billing is offered at a 20% discount for teams that want it, but it’s never required to use the product.
Getting started
Who is Spine for?
Spine is built for founders and operators at SMBs and Series A–C companies — people making a few hires a year where every one of them has to be right. It’s for teams that feel the full cost of a mis-hire and want a second, evidence-based read before they commit.
How fast is Spine to set up?
Minutes, not weeks. You sign up free, connect your hiring tools — Greenhouse, Lever, Gmail or Slack — and Spine builds your pipeline view. You can bring a single live candidate and see Spine’s read next to your own before you make the call.
